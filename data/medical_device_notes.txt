Medical Device Categories — Semiconductor Dependency Assessment
Source: FDA CDRH classifications, Medtronic/Abbott 10-K filings, domain analysis

Top 5 semiconductor-dependent categories:
1. Implantables (pacemakers, neurostimulators) — embedded microcontrollers, irreplaceable once implanted
2. Imaging (MRI, CT, ultrasound) — high-performance processors for real-time image reconstruction
3. Monitoring (ICU monitors, pulse oximeters) — continuous sensor data processing
4. Ventilators — complex embedded control systems, long manufacturing lead times
5. Diagnostics (lab analyzers, PCR machines) — precision analog chips for signal processing

--- Medtronic 10-K (Filed 2026-06-18, Period ending 2026-04-24) ---
Source: SEC EDGAR, CIK 0001613103, mdt-20260424.htm

Key finding: Medtronic explicitly discloses that global semiconductor shortages
"have previously caused, and may in the future cause, disruptions to our product
manufacturing supply chain." Components sourced from sole suppliers in some cases.
No Taiwan-specific sourcing disclosed at this level of detail.

--- Abbott Laboratories 10-K ---
Source: SEC EDGAR, Abbott Laboratories

Key finding: Abbott discloses dependency on "third-party manufacturers and single
suppliers" and global supply chain risks for manufacturing. No explicit semiconductor
or Taiwan mention. Risk disclosure is less specific than Medtronic but confirms
single-source supplier dependency that would be exposed in a blockade scenario.
